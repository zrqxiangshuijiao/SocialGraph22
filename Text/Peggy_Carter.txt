She is usually depicted as a supporting character in books featuring [[Captain America]]. Created by writer [[Stan Lee]] and artist [[Jack Kirby]], she debuted, unnamed, in ''[[Tales of Suspense]]'' #75 as a [[World War II]] love interest of Steve Rogers in [[Flashback (narrative)|flashback]] sequences. She would later be better known as a relative of Captain America's modern-day significant other, [[Sharon Carter]].\n\n[[Hayley Atwell]] portrays the [[Marvel Cinematic Universe]] version of the character, [[Peggy Carter (Marvel Cinematic Universe)|'''Margaret''' \"'''Peggy'''\" '''Carter''']]. (TV series)|What If...?]]'' and the film ''[[Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness]]'' (2022).<ref name=\"Illuminati\" />\n\n==Publication history==\nThe character debuted in a single panel (and unnamed) as a wartime love interest of Captain America in ''[[Tales of Suspense]]'' #75 (March 1966), and then receiving a name in #77 (May 1966). She was created by writer [[Stan Lee]] and artist [[Jack Kirby]].<ref>{{cite web | url=http://www.ign.com/characters/peggy-carter | archive-url=https://web.archive.org/web/20121107224827/http://www.ign.com/characters/peggy-carter | url-status=dead | archive-date=November 7, 2012 | title=Peggy Carter | publisher=[[IGN]] | access-date=21 December 2013}}</ref><ref>[http://www.comics.org/issue/20053/#147873 \"If a Hostage Should Die!\"] {{Webarchive|url=https://web.archive.org/web/20180106065813/http://www.comics.org/issue/20053/#147873 |date=2018-01-06 }}, ''[[Tales of Suspense]]'' #77 (May 1966) at the [[Grand Comics Database]]</ref> She appeared again as the older sister of [[Sharon Carter]] in ''[[Captain America (comic book)|Captain America]]'' #161 (May 1973). She was later [[retcon]]ned as Sharon's aunt, then later great-aunt, due to the [[floating timeline|unaging nature]] of comic book characters (see ''Captain America'' vol. 5 #25 (April 2007)). The character has appeared frequently in Captain America stories set during [[World War II]].{{citation needed|date=February 2014}}\n\n==Fictional character biography==\nDr.<ref>''Marvel Super-Heroes'' vol. 2 #4</ref> Peggy Carter joins the [[French Resistance]] as a teenager and becomes a skilled fighter, who serves on several operations alongside Captain America.<ref>Catherine Saunders, Heather Scott, Julia March, and Alastair Dougall, editors, 2008, ''Marvel Chronicle: A year by Year History'', London: Dorling Kindersley, p. 115; {{ISBN|978-1-4093-8399-4}}.</ref> The two fall in love, but an exploding shell gives her amnesia and she is sent to live with her parents in Virginia.<ref>''Tales of Suspense'' #77. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nDuring the \"[[Original Sin (comics)|Original Sin]]\" storyline, it was revealed in 1952 that Peggy Carter worked with [[Howard Stark]] and Woody McCord when they investigated an alien ship in Siberia.<ref name=\"Operation S.I.N.\">''Operation S.I.N.'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref> The three of them worked to keep the alien from being taken by [[Hydra (comics)|Hydra]] and had the alien live with [[Crimson Dynamo|Anton Vanko]].<ref>''Operation S.I.N.'' #2-5. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nIn the 1960s, Peggy Carter joined up with [[S.H.I.E.L.D.]] for a long tenure.<ref>''Agent Carter: S.H.I.E.L.D. Anniversary'' #1. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nAt the time when Captain America resurfaced in the world, Peggy Carter was taking treatments from [[Doctor Faustus (comics)|Doctor Faustus]]. When she was rescued by Captain America, she maintained her friendship with him.<ref>''Captain America'' #161-163. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nPeggy Carter later helped Captain America fight the [[Secret Empire (organization)|Secret Empire]].<ref>''Captain America'' #174-175. Marvel Comics.</ref> She also dealt with his decision to stop being Captain America for a while.<ref>''Captain America'' #176. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nPeggy helps Captain America when her niece Sharon Carter and some S.H.I.E.L.D. Agents went missing while gathering information on the new Grand Director.<ref>''Captain America'' #231-236. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nPeggy Carter later joined the [[Avengers (comics)|Avengers]]' support staff at [[Avengers Mansion]].<ref>''The Avengers'' #300. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nDuring her retirement, Peggy lived in a nursing home and started suffering dementia where she couldn't recognize Sharon. William Burnside later visited Peggy claiming to be the original Captain America.<ref>''Captain America'' vol. 5 #45. Marvel Comics.</ref> Following her death,<ref name=\"Operation S.I.N.\"/> S.H.I.E.L.D. erected a memorial statue of her outside the S.H.I.E.L.D. Academy in Newark.<ref>''Silk'' vol. 2 #8. Marvel Comics.</ref> Steve Rogers, [[Nick Fury]], and [[Dum Dum Dugan]] attended her funeral.\n\nThrough unknown means, Peggy Carter was revived and rejuvenated where she joined Sharon in forming the Daughters of Liberty under the alias of Dryad. Besides Sharon, the only other people who knew were Falcon and Winter Soldier.<ref name=\"Captain America #19\">''Captain America'' vol. 9 #19. Marvel Comics.</ref> She assisted the group in working to clear Captain America's name when he was framed for the death of [[Thunderbolt Ross]]. Dryad fought Ross' real murder [[Foreigner (comics)|Foreigner]] until Crossbones and Sin fired a missile at them. Dryad survived while Foreigner presumably perishes.<ref>''Captain America'' vol. 9 #9-11. Marvel Comics.</ref> She secretly assists [[Bucky Barnes|Winter Soldier]] into feeding false intel on Captain America's whereabouts to [[Nick Fury Jr.]] in order to throw him off. After getting some intel from Baron Strucker's mind, Peggy's location is stormed by Nick Fury Jr. and his men while Peggy was asking Sharon when she is going to tell Captain America about her revival. Peggy tells Nick Fury Jr. that he and his men will have to go through her first if they want to get to Captain America. Peggy subdues the soldiers and holds Nick Fury Jr. at gunpoint in order to straighten him out.<ref>''Captain America'' vol. 9 #12-13. Marvel Comics.</ref> Deciding it's time to let Steve know about her return, Peggy arranged for Daughters of Liberty teammate [[Agatha Harkness]] to bring Steve to her. She revealed to him that she has been tracking Aleksander Lukin ever since the event where Lukin and Selene took a soul fragment from Sharon. In addition, Peggy stated to Steve that Lukin was behind the death of Thunderbolt Ross, the attacks of the Watchdogs, and the new [[Scourge of the Underworld|Scourge]]. Sharon, Falcon, and Winter Soldier enter while explaining why they kept Peggy's revival a secret. Peggy and Winter Soldier reveal to Steve that the soul fragment that manifested as a stone was used to revive Lukin which also revived the remnants of Red Skull's mind that is in him.<ref name=\"Captain America #19\"/>\n\n==Abilities==\nPeggy Carter is shown to be a superb hand-to-hand combatant and martial artist, also excelling in using firearms. She is a highly trained spy, tactician, strategist, and commander.\n\nAs Dryad, Peggy wears a type of battle armor that is strong enough to protect her from a missile attack and keep her insulated when it is ablaze.\n\n==Other versions==\n===Amalgam Comics===\nIn ''Super-Soldier: Man of War'' #1 from [[Amalgam Comics]], '''Mademoiselle Peggy''' is a cross between Peggy Carter and [[DC Comics]]' World War II version of [[Mademoiselle Marie]].<ref>''Super-Soldier: Man of War'' #1 (June 1997).
