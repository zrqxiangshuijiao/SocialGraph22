Marvel Comics.</ref> With the aid of the traitorous magician Igron, Mangog assumes the form of an imprisoned Odin, and intends to once again draw the Odinsword. Marvel Comics.</ref>\n\nThe character reappears twice in the second volume of ''Thor'': as the servant of a clone of the Titan [[Thanos]] (destroyed by Thor)<ref>''Thor'' vol. 2 #20-25 (Feb.-July 2000). Marvel Comics.</ref> and in a desolated Asgard during [[Ragnarok]] (dispelled by Thor with the Odinforce)<ref>''Thor'' vol. 2 #84 (Nov. 2004). Marvel Comics.</ref> Mangog reappears in the ''Thunderstrike'' mini-series when accidentally summoned by a mystical generator, and is eventually defeated when cast into a distant star.<ref>''Thunderstrike'' vol. 2 #5 (June 2011)</ref>\n\nReappearing in ''The Mighty Thor'', Mangog decimates Asgard until he was hurled by [[Jane Foster (comics)|Jane Foster]] (the new Thor) into the sun.<ref>''The Mighty Thor'' #705 (Feb. 2018).
